Top Univerisites Around The World

This visualisation shows the top universities around the world based on 5 criteria, Teaching score, International score, International score, citations score and income score. The overall ranking of each univeristy is based on the 5 criteria.

Teaching

The teaching criteria is based on the learning environment and the teaching capabilities of the teachers within each university. The bar chart to the right depicts the top 4 universities in the world in relation to their teaching score. California Institute of Technology (CalTech) ranks the highest, Harvard University ranking second, Standford University Ranking third and Massachusetts Institute of Technology ranking fourth.

International

The international outlook criteria is based on each universities diversity of staff, students and research and how they cater for every religion and culture. The bar chart on the left shows top 7 univerisites in relation to the international score. The following Universities ranked the highest with a score of 100, University of Macau, Qatar Univeristy, University of Geneva, University of Hong Kong, University of Innsbruck, University of Luxembourg and London school of Econmonics.

Research

The research criteria takes in to account the volume, income and reputation of each universities research. The bar chart to the displays the top 12 universities based on their research score. Imperial College of London ranks the highest, whereas, California Institute of technology (Caltech) ranks 12. Princeton Univeristy, University of Cambridge, University of California, Brakeley and Massachusetts Institute of Technology rounds out the top 5.

Citations

The citations criteria is all about each universities research influence citation. The bar chart depicts the top 7 universities around the world in relation to the citations score. Massachusetts Institute of Technology ranks the highest with Tokyo Metropolitan University ranking second. Princeton University, National Research Nulcear University, Rice University, University of California, Santa Cruz and St George's University of London all rank tied third.

Income

The income criteria is based on each Universitites Industry Income which is related to thier knowledge transfer. This bar chart shows the top 9 Universities around the world in relation to their income score. Peking University and KU Leuven ranked the highest, with Swedish University of Agriculture placing third. Eindhoven University of Technology, Nanyang Technological University, National Cheng Kung University, tsinghua University and University of Basel all have the same income score ranking of fourth.

Ranking

The Ranking score takes in to account the scores of the 5 criteria, teaching, international, research, citations and income and ranks each Univeristy in relation to each other. The bar chart displays the top 12 ranked Universities around the world. Imperial College of London Ranks the highest, Princeton University Ranks second and University of Cambridge ranks third. These rankings suggest that not having the highest score in one criteria relates to a good University ranking.

Ranking vs Population

The bubble plot to the right shows and compares the ranking of each university to thier corresponding University population. This plot suggests that the highest ranked universities don't always have the highest population, meaning population does not correlate and cause Universities to have a high University world ranking.
